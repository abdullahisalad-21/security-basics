# Principle of Least Privilege

## Overview
Least privilege means giving users only the access they need — nothing more. This reduces risk and prevents accidental or malicious damage.

---

## 1. Why It Matters
- Reduces attack surface  
- Prevents accidental system changes  
- Limits malware impact  
- Protects sensitive data  

---

## 2. Examples from Practice
- Running PowerShell as admin only when required  
- Using `sudo` for specific Linux commands  
- Removing unnecessary group memberships  
- Fixing “Access Denied” by adjusting permissions, not elevating rights  

---

- How to avoid unnecessary admin rights
